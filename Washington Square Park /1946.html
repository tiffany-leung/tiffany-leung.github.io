Life in the 1940s

This is the time when the world just came out of the trauma of WWII. After WWII officially ended in 1945, the war had left countries in states of devastation. Cities and towns were destroyed into rubble, and populations were massacred. Fortunately, the United States was not one of those countries that had to suffer much loss. However, this war had lost them a lot of young men who enlisted into the United States army at their youth. The returning veterans were given jobs and college educations under the G.I. Bill, but as much as the United States goverment tried to help the veterans ease back into society like before, a lot of them suffered from PTSD (Post Traumatic Stress Disorder) or were unable to return to their previous lives.
