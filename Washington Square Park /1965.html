Life in the 1960s

The 1960s was the age of the youth and change. Children born in the Baby Boom Era have grown up, living in a society that has moved on from the times of WWII. The large population of youths in the United States changed the direction of design and visual culture, as well as the emergence of Rock and Roll and new social movements. Besides the movements for "sex, drugs and rock & roll", the 60s was also the decade of change. The African Americans pushed for equality in the Civil Rights Movement, and women also started fighting for equal pay and opportunities in the work force. Some protests also went into international matters, such as the protests against the war in Vietnam. Therefore the 60s was remembered as the turning point for America - the time when society started being different.
