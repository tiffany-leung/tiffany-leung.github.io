Life in the 1970s

In some ways the 1970s was a continuation of the 1960s, or even more tumultuous. People such as Women, African Americans, Gays, Lesbians and marginalized people contiuned to fight for equality. Ongoing protests against the War in Vietnam continued to happen, until the war ended in 1974. A “New Right” mobilized in defense of political conservatism and traditional family roles, and the behavior of President Richard Nixon undermined many people’s faith in the good intentions of the federal government. The Environmental Movement flourished in this decade, when people started caring about protecting the environment from toxic industrial wastes and meltdowns at nuclear power plants. Americans celebrated the first Earth Day in 1970, and the Congressed passed the National Environmental Policy act in the same year.
