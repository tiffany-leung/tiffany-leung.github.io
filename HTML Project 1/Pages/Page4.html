There is nothing wrong with my accent

part iv

I spent 6 years of my secondary education in international schools and also studying in the US right now. There is no doubt that I have picked up a very distinctly foreign (or some people label it as "ang mor") accent; it is even very difficult for me to go back to my original Singaporean accent. But I am NOT being fake. Some people might say, "If you are really Singaporean, you should speak in a Singaporean accent", and I can rebuttal that with, "If you are really Chinese, you should speak Mandarin in the classic Chinese accent" . Wouldn't that be fair?
